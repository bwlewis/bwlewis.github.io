Example 1   2 × 3
  1. Move the C-scale's left index over the first factor (2) on the D-scale.
  2. Locate the second factor (3) on the C-scale using the hairline on the cursor and read the answer directly below on the D-scale.
The slide rule works out the solution by computing: 2 × 3 = 10log(2) × 10log(3) = 10log(2)+log(3). Note that decimal places are arbitrary. This example is equivalent to computing 2 × 30 = 60 and 0.2 × 0.3 = 0.06, etc.

The division problem 6 ÷ 3 = 2 is carried out in the reverse way:

  1. Position the first factor in the division problem (the 6) on the D-scale directly below the second factor (3) on the C-scale.
  2. Read the answer on the D-scale directly below the left index on the C-scale.
The B-scale is displays the squares of the C-scale values. An equivalent statement of this example is 2 × sqrt(9). Move the left index on the C-scale over the first factor 2 on the D-scale. Then locate the square of the second factor (9) on the B-scale with the cursor's hairline and read the answer on the D-scale.
