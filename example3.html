Example 3   15 × sqrt(2) × 3.3
It's easy to chain multiple computations together.
  1. Position the left index on the C-scale over the first factor (the 15) on the D-scale.
  2. Locate the cursor's hairline over the 2 on the B-scale (to multiply by the square root of 2).
  3. Position the left index on the C-scale on the hairline.
  4. Locate the cursor's hairline over the next factor (3.3) on the C-scale and read the answer directly below on the D-scale.

This approach may be used to string any number of multiplications (and divisions) together.
